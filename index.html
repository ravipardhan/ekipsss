Our Core Products

Control Transformer

A transformer that is used to step down the voltage to power the control devices of a circuit or machine.

View Details

Three Phase Transformer

Passive machines that pass electrical energy between circuits, stepping up or stepping down voltages without altering the frequency.

View Details

Buck Boost Transformer

Works by taking input voltage from the primary side and stepping it up or down depending on the preferred configuration.

View Details

Lift UPS 3-Phase Transformer

Feeds for Door closure controls, DC Braking, SMPS module, Illumination, ARD which operates with high frequency & 3 phase low voltage mode.

View Details

UPS and Ard Transformers

Used to step up the AC voltage levels, protect the UPS from load disruptions and provide galvanic isolation.

View Details

Sine Wave Transformers

A sine wave is a continuous wave that describes a smooth repetitive oscillation. Conventional AC power is produced by rotating machines.

View Details

Line Choke

Used to provide improved protection against overvoltages on the line supply and to reduce harmonic distortion of the current.

View Details

Inverter Transformer

In power conversion applications an inverter is an electronic transformer which converts power from a Direct Current (D.C) source into Alternating Current (A.C) power. Power conversion inverters can be divided into two sub-categories, voltage-fed and current-fed inverters.

View Details

Auto Transformers

An autotransformer is an electrical transformer with only one winding. The "auto" prefix refers to the single coil acting alone, not to any kind of automatic mechanism. In an autotransformer, portions of the same winding act as both the primary winding and secondary winding sides of the transformer.

View Details

Isolation Transformer

Ensures complete galvanic isolation for noise reduction and safety, protecting sensitive electronic equipment from voltage spikes.
