Maintenance Guide

Essential Transformer Maintenance Tips for Longevity

Published by Ekips Transformers Team

Transformers are the heart of any electrical distribution system. While they are designed to be robust and long-lasting, neglect can lead to efficiency losses, unexpected failures, and costly downtime. A proactive maintenance schedule is essential to ensure your equipment operates safely and efficiently for years to come.

1. Regular Oil Analysis (DGA)

For oil-cooled transformers, the condition of the insulating oil is a direct indicator of the transformer's health. Dissolved Gas Analysis (DGA) is a crucial test that detects gases generated by internal faults such as overheating or arcing.

  • Check Dielectric Strength: Ensure the oil can withstand electrical stress.
  • Moisture Content: Water in oil reduces insulation properties drastically.
  • Acidity: High acidity can cause sludge formation and corrosion.

2. Visual Inspections

A simple visual check can reveal a lot about a transformer's condition. Walk around the unit and look for:

  • Oil Leaks: Check gaskets, valves, and radiators for any signs of seepage.
  • Silica Gel Breather: The silica gel should be blue (or orange, depending on the type). If it has turned pink or white, it is saturated with moisture and needs replacing or reactivating.
  • Physical Damage: Look for dents, rust, or damage to bushings and insulators.

3. IR (Insulation Resistance) Testing

Over time, the insulation between windings can degrade due to heat, moisture, and aging. An IR test (Megger test) measures the resistance of the insulation. A significant drop in resistance values compared to historical data indicates potential insulation failure.

4. Keep it Clean and Cool

Dust, dirt, and debris can accumulate on the transformer's surface and cooling fins (radiators). This layer acts as an insulator, preventing heat from dissipating effectively. Overheating is one of the leading causes of transformer failure.

Ensure that the cooling fans (if equipped) are operational and that the area around the transformer is free from obstructions to allow for proper airflow.

Conclusion

Preventive maintenance is always more cost-effective than reactive repairs. By following these simple steps and scheduling regular professional check-ups with Ekips Transformers, you can significantly extend the lifespan of your assets and ensure uninterrupted power for your operations.
