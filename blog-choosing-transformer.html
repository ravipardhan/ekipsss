How to Choose the Right Transformer for Your Industry

Published by Ekips Transformers Team

Selecting the correct transformer is critical for the safety, efficiency, and reliability of your electrical system. An undersized transformer can lead to overheating and failure, while an oversized one results in unnecessary capital expenditure and energy losses. Here is a guide to help you make an informed decision.

1. Determine the Load Requirement (kVA)

The first step is to calculate the total load the transformer needs to support. This is measured in kVA (Kilovolt-Amperes). You should sum up the power consumption of all equipment that will be connected. It is standard practice to add a 20% safety margin to account for future expansion and peak loads.

2. Input and Output Voltages

You must know the primary voltage (the source voltage from the grid or generator) and the secondary voltage (the voltage required by your equipment). Common industrial voltages include 415V, 440V, and 11kV, while equipment might need 230V or 110V.

3. Type of Load: Linear vs. Non-Linear

This is a crucial but often overlooked factor.

  • Linear Loads: Standard motors, heaters, and incandescent lighting. Standard transformers work well here.
  • Non-Linear Loads: Computers, VFDs (Variable Frequency Drives), and LED lighting. These generate harmonics which cause heating. For these applications, you need a K-Rated Transformer or an Isolation Transformer to handle the harmonic distortion.

4. Environmental Conditions

Where will the transformer be installed?

  • Indoor: Dry-type air-cooled transformers are usually preferred for indoor use as they are safer and require less maintenance.
  • Outdoor: Oil-cooled transformers are robust and better suited for outdoor environments but require containment for potential oil leaks.

Conclusion

Choosing the right transformer involves balancing electrical requirements, environmental factors, and budget. At Ekips Transformers, we specialize in custom-building transformers to meet your exact specifications.
